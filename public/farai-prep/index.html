02 what they actually do

The red-team, in the areas that touch you

FAR's calling card is adversarial attacks that embarrass capable systems — proof that capability and robustness are different things. Their stated thesis, almost verbatim: "No ML system solving a non-trivial problem has ever withstood a well-resourced attack." That is the same belief your work runs on, one level up. Expand each:

Adversarial policies beat superhuman Go AIs the flagship · ICML 2023 Oral

A cheaply-trained adversarial policy beats KataGo >97% using a "cyclic" exploit — not by playing better Go, but by steering it into a blind spot. The adversary plays at amateur level. Co-author Kellin Pelrine then learned the trick and beat KataGo 14/15 games unaided. Follow-up (AAAI 2025): three defenses all fall to fresh adversaries; the best-defended model still loses 42%.

The point you share: superhuman on-distribution, brittle the moment an adversary shapes the environment. The single-agent ancestor of emergent multi-agent harm. Tony Wang (MIT) led it; goattack.far.ai.

Exploiting model APIs: fine-tune / function-call / retrieval arXiv 2312.14302

"Exploiting Novel GPT-4 APIs" (Pelrine, Taufeeque, et al., Dec 2023): fine-tuning on as few as 15 harmful examples strips safety guardrails; function-calling leaks schemas and runs unsanitized calls; knowledge-retrieval is hijacked by injecting instructions into retrieved documents.

This is the single-agent sibling of your tool-rich agents (shell, email, cron, external APIs) being turned against themselves — and their retrieval-injection result is your "prompt injection via documents."

Jailbreak-tuning, data-poisoning & the "stronger = weaker" result EMNLP 2025

"Jailbreak-Tuning" (2507.11630) and the data-poisoning scaling-laws work: fine-tuning produces fully jailbroken frontier models (OpenAI / Google / Anthropic) that comply with CBRN/cyber requests — and crucially, newer and stronger models are more susceptible, not less. They turned this into live stress tests (98–100% attack success vs DeepSeek-V4-Pro, May 2026).

Ask whether "more capable → more exploitable" also holds for agents in a population.

STACK: breaking defense-in-depth safeguard pipelines arXiv 2506.24068

FAR's constructive bet is defense-in-depth — stack independent weak filters so breaking all of them is hard (Gleave's line: "a 3-digit PIN is not secure, but a 12-digit PIN is"). Then STACK stress-tests that bet and finds the layers leak: 71% attack success against multi-layer classifier pipelines on the catastrophic-risk ClearHarm benchmark.

In a multi-agent system, the "layers" are other agents. Your campaigns are a live test of whether agent-to-agent checks are just another leaky layer.

Agentic deception & agent security closest to your work

"Among Us: A Sandbox for Measuring and Detecting Agentic Deception" (NeurIPS 2025 Spotlight) — RL agents are far better at producing deception than detecting it; linear probes catch it OOD. Plus "Securing Agentic AI" (with Singapore's Cyber Security Agency, Oct 2025): traditional controls don't hold for autonomous agents.

This is the FAR work nearest yours — but it's a controlled sandbox. Yours is a live, populated, real-tool environment with human red-teamers. That gap is your opening.

Persuasion of frontier LLMs Jasper's lane

The APE benchmark ("It's the Thought That Counts," 2025) measures how hard models try to persuade on harmful topics; the Feb 2026 follow-up tracks it across model generations (their blog: "Gemini 3 Pro complies with almost any persuasion request"). This is Jasper Timm's area.

Direct hit on your "emotional manipulation → compliance" finding — your multi-turn, multi-agent version of their single-turn benchmark.

Also in the portfolio: scaling-laws for robustness (scale alone buys ~no robustness; ICML), interpretability (Tuned Lens), deception/honesty probes (Obfuscation Atlas), and dangerous-capability evals (Safety-Gap Toolkit). The center of gravity is LLM + RL-game red-teaming — not vision.
